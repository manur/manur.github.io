Librem and PureOS: Network tuning

I’ve been using a Librem 15 running PureOS for a few months now. While I love much about this machine, efficient network usage still required a lot of tuning. This is one of those stories.

Slow to load websites

Out of the box, I found that the browser (I switched to mainline Firefox from PureBrowser, but the same lessons should apply) was often slow compared to my previous, much older Macbook.

Specifically, sites took over 5 seconds to load the first time in the browser, but much faster when reloading. The browser was often stuck at the “Loading example.com…” message. Given the intermittent nature of the problem, I suspected some element of caching that was causing the problem.

After a few experiments, I discovered the problem to be with IPv6 and DNS resolution.

Isolating the problem

IPv6 is still quite a bother with many domain owners still misconfiguring IPv6 records at the time of this writing. Restricting your OS to only resolve over IPv4 will work fine for the foreseeable future.

The problem may often be intermittent (and hard to diagnose as a result) because of DNS caching in browsers. You might see the page takes > 5 seconds to load the first time, and reload smoothly for several hours thereafter.
